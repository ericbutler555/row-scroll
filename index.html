Hey

This is RowScroll.
Scroll or swipe down to learn more.

1

Tested on Chrome (Mac and Windows), Firefox (Mac and Windows), IE9/10/11 (Windows), and Safari (Mac).
Works on all.

2

Set your own row/section heights in your own CSS.
Can be the full viewport height (as shown here), or a fixed pixel height, whatever.

3

Row heights can vary.
The row elements don't have to all be the same height, they can all be different if you want. Doesn't matter.

4

The element isn't important, either.
Use <div>, <section>, or any other element you prefer for your row containers.
You can even mix and match.

5

Just add class="scroll-target" to each row container you want your visitors to auto-scroll to.

6

That's it!

7

Just remember to load jQuery and jQuery Mousewheel before you include RowScroll:

  <script src="http://code.jquery.com/jquery-1.11.3.min.js"></script>
  <script src="http://cdnjs.cloudflare.com/ajax/libs/jquery-mousewheel/3.1.13/jquery.mousewheel.min.js"></script>
  <script src="your/path/to/rowScroll.min.js"></script>
</body>

8
